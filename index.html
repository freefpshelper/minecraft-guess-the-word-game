what is fps

In the fast-paced world of gaming, every frame counts. Whether you're immersed
in an intense first-person shooter, navigating complex open-world landscapes, or
engaging in competitive eSports, the smoothness of your gameplay can make or break
your experience. That’s where FPS Frames Per Second comes in.

FPS measures how many frames your system can render per second while running a game.
A higher FPS means smoother visuals, better responsiveness, and a more immersive gaming experience.
On the flip side, low FPS can lead to choppy gameplay, input lag, and even headaches from visual
stuttering a nightmare for both casual and competitive players.



But what exactly affects FPS?

At its core, FPS is influenced by your system’s hardware, including your graphics card (GPU),
processor (CPU), RAM, and storage speed. But it’s not just about expensive upgrades.
Your game settings, background applications, driver versions, and even the resolution
you're playing at can have a significant impact on performance.

System Restore Point

befor you do anything allways set a System Restore Point on your pc. System Restore Point on your PC acts as
a safety net for your files, settings, and overall system. Whether you’re a gamer, a student,
or someone who relies on your computer for work, unexpected issues can strike at any time —
from software crashes to accidental file deletions. By creating a System Restore Point, often called a
“restore point” or “backup,” you give yourself the ability to roll back your system to a stable,
working state.

For gamers, System Restore Point mean you can recover progress in case of a crash or corrupted data.
In everyday PC use, a system restore point can save hours of troubleshooting if an update,
driver change, or software install causes problems. Instead of losing work or reinstalling
Windows, you simply revert to the last known good configuration.

System Restore Point are quick to make and take up minimal space compared to the value they offer.
Think of them as checkpoints in a game — you don’t notice them when things are going well,
but you’ll be grateful they exist when something goes wrong. Making it a habit to create regular
System Restore Point keeps your PC safer and your mind at ease.
